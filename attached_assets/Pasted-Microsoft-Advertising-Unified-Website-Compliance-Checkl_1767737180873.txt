Microsoft Advertising
Unified Website Compliance Checklist (Priority-Based)
Use this as your single source of truth for all current and future websites.

🔴 CRITICAL (Must Fix First – Ad Approval Blockers)
1. Business Identity & Transparency
Goal: Clearly show who operates the business.
Required
Display the legal business name in:
Footer
Contact page
About page
Show a real physical address on the website.
If the address is residential or non-storefront, add clarification:
“Corporate mailing address” OR
“Business warehouse and shipping address”
Provide clear contact options:
Email (mandatory)
Phone (recommended)
If no phone is available, add:
“Phone support is currently unavailable. Please contact us via email.”


2. Legal & Policy Pages (Mandatory for Ads)
Goal: Policies must reflect real business operations.
Pages required
5. Privacy Policy
6. Terms of Service
7. Shipping Policy
8. Return & Refund Policy
9. Disclaimer
Each policy must include
10. Business name
11. Contact email
12. Country/state
13. “Last updated” date
Privacy Policy must disclose
14. Data collection methods
15. Cookie usage
16. Analytics tools
17. Advertising / tracking tools
18. Email marketing practices and opt-out rights
Shipping Policy must explain
19. Processing times
20. Shipping carriers
21. Cutoff times
22. Handling periods
23. Peak-season delays

3. Homepage Content Strength
Goal: Avoid “thin” or placeholder site flags.
Clear introduction explaining:
What the site is
What it offers
Who it is for
Minimum 300–500 words of original homepage content
At least one section such as:
Featured guides
Popular topics
Helpful resources

4. Thin Page Fixes (High-Risk Area)
Goal: Eliminate low-value or empty pages.
Any short page must either:
Be expanded to 250–300+ words, OR
Be removed from:
Menu
Sitemap
Internal links
Applies to
28. Category pages
29. Tag pages
30. Troubleshooting pages
31. Resource hubs

🟠 HIGH PRIORITY (Strongly Affects Trust & Manual Review)
5. Product Accuracy & Brand Representation
Goal: Avoid misleading brand claims.
Remove unsupported phrases such as:
❌ “genuine HP products” (unless reseller proof exists)
For OEM products:
Show Manufacturer Part Number (MPN) OR
Display authorized reseller proof
For compatible products, clearly state:
“Compatible with [Brand/Model]”
Do not imply official affiliation unless documented.

6. HP Printer Product Page Rules (If Applicable)
Apply only to printer products (not supplies).
Add near specifications:
“OEM Warranty Included”
Auto-detect and display:
Manufacturer Part Number (MPN): HP Product Number
Place MPN:
Under the model name or inside the specifications block
Do NOT
40. Add MPN or OEM warranty text to supplies
41. Change any product images
42. Use “genuine HP products” wording anywhere

7. Return, Refund & RMA Policy Clarity
Goal: Remove ambiguity.
Replace unclear fee ranges (e.g., “15–25%”).
Use:
“Up to X%” OR clearly defined tier rules
Ensure free-return claims appear consistently across:
Ads
Product pages
Category pages
Clearly define RMA shipping responsibility:
Prepaid label OR customer-paid return

8. About Us Page (Trust-Critical)
Business name
Purpose of the website
What the business offers
Operating location (city/state/country)
Who operates the site (individual or team)
Mission or goal

🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY (Improves Approval Stability & Account Health)
9. Real Value Content Standards
Goal: Avoid “thin affiliate” classification.
Add original content such as:
Buying guides
Product comparisons
How-to guides
Expert advice
Safety tips
Target 300–600 words per core page
Avoid copied manufacturer descriptions

10. Product & Category Page Expansion
Detailed descriptions
Benefits and drawbacks
Use cases
Safety information
Comparison notes
Original images when possible
Avoid one-paragraph listings

11. Trust Signals & External Credibility
Real payment processor badges (only if used)
SSL / security badges
Shipping carrier logos
Verified reviews including:
Date
Name or initials
Order reference pattern
Optional
67. EIN or state registration reference
68. Real business photos
69. Author or owner bios

🟢 LOW PRIORITY (Required but Easy Wins)
12. Newsletter & Email Transparency
Add under signup forms:
“By subscribing, you agree to receive promotional emails. You can unsubscribe anytime.”
Ensure Privacy Policy reflects email marketing and unsubscribe rights

13. Affiliate Disclosure (If Applicable)
Place visible disclosure on relevant pages:
“This website participates in affiliate programs. We may earn a commission from qualifying purchases at no extra cost to you.”
